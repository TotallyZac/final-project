Judy Garland

Personal Life

Childhood

Judy was born on June 10th, 1922 in Grand Rapids, Minnesota. Judy was just two and a half years old when she first performed on stage. She mentioned that being on stage was the only time she felt wanted. Her mother was for sure a stage mom. Judy was not a wanted child. As her parents were trying for an abortion which was illegal at the time. Judy and her family moved to California in 1926. That opened up more opportunities to Judy.

Teenage Years

Judy was brought by her mother to many gigs. Her mother was constantly pushing her into numerous things. Her mother treated her very poorly. She would give Judy pills to make sure she would have energy and to sleep. Judy was not even 10 years old at the time. With Judy having a contract to MGM her appearance was quickly judged. She was put on extreme diets and was being told to take diet pills.

Later Years

After Judy's teenage years things did not get much better. On a positive note, she had three healthy children and one of them which most probably know is Liza Minelli. Judy struggled financially and mentally with being married five times and having money troubles it really took a toll on Judy. She died on June 22nd, 1969 from an accidental overdose.

Notable Work

The Wizard of Oz

Judy starred as Dorothy Gale the main character from Kansas who lands in a special place called Oz. She makes new friends and enemies on the way to the Wizard. Many are unaware of how Judy was treated during this movie. She was paid the worst, one of the lowest paid on the movie. Reports of her being abused, the diet pills were coming into play.

A Star is Born

Hollywood actor Norman Maine is a celebrity whose star is on the wane, but when he meets aspiring actress Esther Blodgett, he is inspired to help her, and soon the two appear in a musical together. Now known as Vicki Lester, she marries Norman and finds herself in demand, while his reputation continues to decline, resulting in heavy bouts of drinking. Eventually, Vicki must choose between moving forward with her career and attempting to save her husband.

Meet Me in St. Louis

"Meet Me in St. Louis" is a classic MGM romantic musical comedy that focuses on four sisters (one of whom is the nonpareil Judy Garland) on the cusp of the 1904 St. Louis World's Fair. The film spotlights the sisters' education in the ways of the world, which includes, but isn't limited to, learning about life and love, courtesy of the prototypical boy next door. In the end, love -- accompanied by song, dance and period costumes, all in glorious Technicolor -- conquers all.
